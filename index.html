Riptide Public Projects

B-Linepack+ Ofgem SIF Funded Project

Future hydrogen gas networks will require additional storage to supplement linepack. Riptide successfully delivered the engineering design for the B-Linepack+ solution on behalf of Longitude Engineering as part of a £500k Ofgem SIF funded project led by National Gas Transmissions. This included the mechanical, structural, process and systems engineering of the novel sub-surface Lined Rock Shaft hydrogen storage technology, encompassing design, risk management, project planning, cost modelling and the grant application.

Previous Experience

Worlds First Gravity Energy Storage Device

Steven led the technical delivery of the world's first gravity energy storage device whilst working at Gravitricity. This involved the design, build, commissioning, testing and decommissioning of a 250KW demonstrator in Edinburgh. The system allowed the team to successfully validate the safety, performance and capability of the technology to provides energy storage to facilitate the uptake of renewable energy production. It led to many feasibility studies and funding opportunities for Gravitricity to take the technology to commercialisation.

Novel Underground Hydrogen Storage

Jack and Steven led the technology development of the mid-scale underground hydrogen storage technology H2FlexiStore at Gravitricity. They took the multi-disciplinary solution from idea to concept design maturity, ready to begin materials testing, design studies and numerical modelling investigations. Understanding how to bring new technology to fruition was critical, considering the novel technology, storing a highly flammable hazard gas. They built risk management systems, developed engineering strategy, and created a robust technical roadmap for progression.

Product Engineering Design at Dyson

Jack worked at Dyson delivering the engineering design for multiple new and novel wearable products including the Dyson Zone pictured. When developing solutions for problems no one has yet solved, innovation and creativity is key. Working to tight timelines, costs and product requirements, Jack had to develop designs for key product sub-systems, upholding Dyson’s reputation for quality engineering and robust reliable products. He worked with teams, suppliers and manufacturing teams from around the world to create market leading solutions.

iF Award winning Future Systems Simulator

Jack developed the initial design proposal for the Future Systems Simulator for Rolls-Royce which then went on to win an IF Design Award for Product User Experience (UX). The simulator is in regular use at the Aerospcae Integration Research Centre at Cranfield University. It is designed to facilitate the simulation of future cockpit systems and setups to improve the safety and performance of flight. The work required multi-disciplinary technical and industrial design for a new approach to innovation in aerospace.

Crossrail Training Mock-up Design & Build

DCA delivered a six meter exterior and interior visually representative training mock-up for the Tunnelling and Underground Construction Academy as part of Crossrail’s training program for the Elizabeth Line in London. Jack supported the work by designing actuating mechanisms, which were manufactured and assembled as part of the final mock-up. This involved design simulations, design for manufacture, part assembly and testing. The mock-up was received by the training centre and formed a key part of their program.
